# Astoria

![Astoria and the Astoria–Megler Bridge over the Columbia River](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1a/Astoria%2C_Oregon.jpg)
*Photo: M.O. Stevens / Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 3.0*

**Day trip from Manzanita — 1h30m north.** Astoria isn't a beach town — it's a working river city at the mouth of the Columbia, the oldest American settlement west of the Rockies (founded 1811). Think Victorian mansions, an excellent craft-beer and food scene, the *Goonies* house, and a 6-mile riverwalk with a vintage trolley. Worth a full day if you want a break from beach and sand.

**Distance from Manzanita:** ~1h30m north on US-101
**Vibe**: Historic city, craft breweries, riverfront

**Last updated**: 2026-07-14

## Restaurants & Bars

Astoria's food scene is the real reason to come — it punches far above a town of 10,000.

### Breweries (Astoria's Claim to Fame)

**Fort George Brewery & Public House** ★ — *the standout, don't miss*
- **Cuisine:** New American brewpub, wood-fired pizza, house-brewed beer
- **Address:** 1483 Duane St · [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Fort+George+Brewery+1483+Duane+St+Astoria+OR)
- **Phone:** (503) 325-7468 · **Web:** [fortgeorgebrewery.com](https://fortgeorgebrewery.com/)
- **Hours:** Daily 11 AM–10 PM
- **Price:** $$ · **No reservations** (grab a table, order at counter upstairs; full-service downstairs)
- **Must-order:** The Vortex IPA and 3-Way IPA (flagships), the sweet-potato fries, and a wood-fired pizza. Take a free brewery peek. Occupies a restored 1920s auto building — two floors, great atmosphere.

**Buoy Beer Company** — *iconic setting*
- **Cuisine:** Brewpub, seafood, burgers, house lagers
- **Address:** 1343 Duane St (Duane St taproom) · [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Buoy+Beer+1343+Duane+St+Astoria+OR)
- **Web:** [buoybeer.com](https://buoybeer.com/) · **Hours:** Sun 11–8, Mon–Thu 12–8, Fri 12–9, Sat 11–9
- **Price:** $$
- **Note:** The famous **original riverfront brewpub on Marine Dr partially collapsed into the river in 2022** and remains closed/under repair. Operations moved to the Duane St location and the Food Hub — still great beer, but the sea-lion-under-the-glass-floor experience is on hold. Check current status before going for the view.
- **Must-order:** Czech-style pilsner, fish & chips.

**Rogue Ales Public House (Pier 39)** — *waterfront on a historic cannery pier*
- **Address:** 100 39th St, Pier 39 · **Phone:** (503) 325-5964
- **Hours:** Sun–Thu 11 AM–9 PM, Fri–Sat 11 AM–10 PM · **Price:** $$
- **Why go:** Sits on Pier 39 (the old Bumble Bee cannery) with the **Hanthorn Cannery Museum** (free) next door and a coffee roaster. Dead Guy Ale on tap, out over the river.

**Wet Dog Café / Astoria Brewing Co.** — Astoria's oldest brewery, casual riverfront pub with a big deck. Address: 144 11th St · Burgers, pub grub, house beers. $$

### Seafood & Fine Dining

**Bridgewater Bistro** — *best riverfront fine-ish dining*
- **Cuisine:** New American / Pacific NW seafood · **Address:** 20 Basin St, Ste A (at the marina)
- **Phone:** (503) 325-6777 · **Web:** [bridgewaterbistro.com](https://bridgewaterbistro.com/)
- **Hours:** Mon–Thu 11:30–3 & 4–8:30; Fri–Sat 11:30–3 & 4–9; Sun 10:30–3 (brunch) & 4–8:30
- **Price:** $$$ · **Reservations recommended** (especially dinner)
- **Must-order:** Cedar-plank salmon, seafood chowder, the airy timber-frame room with river views.

**Silver Salmon Grille** — *downtown seafood institution*
- **Address:** 1105 Commercial St · **Phone:** (503) 325-1067 · **Web:** [silversalmongrille.com](https://silversalmongrille.com/)
- **Price:** $$$ · **Reservations recommended** · **Must-order:** the namesake silver salmon dishes, Dungeness crab cakes. Classic white-tablecloth-but-friendly.

**Clemente's** — Pacific seafood, family-run, sources local catch. Downtown, $$–$$$. A local favorite for fresh fish; reservations wise on weekends.

**Columbian Café** — *eccentric, legendary local hole-in-the-wall*
- **Cuisine:** Eclectic, seafood, famous crêpes and house pepper jellies · **Address:** 1114 Marine Dr
- **Price:** $$ · **Cash-friendly, tiny, no-reservations** — expect a wait, part of the charm. A true only-in-Astoria spot; been slinging inventive food for decades. Grab a counter seat.

### Casual & Lunch

**Bowpicker Fish & Chips** ★ — *the "only in Astoria" experience*
- **What:** Beer-battered albacore tuna fish & chips served from a **converted gillnet fishing boat** parked on land, across from the Maritime Museum (17th & Duane area).
- **Cash only. No indoor seating.** Lines form fast; often sells out and closes early. Usually open **Wed–Sun, ~11 AM–close (or sellout)** — hours are famously loose, so go early.
- **Price:** $ · **Must-order:** the only thing they make — one- or two-piece tuna & chips. Worth the line.

**Drina Daisy** — *Bosnian, genuinely unique on the coast*
- **Address:** 915 Commercial St · **Phone:** (503) 338-2912
- **Price:** $$ · **Must-order:** the spit-roasted lamb (call ahead — it sells out), cevapi, Balkan pastries. Warm, personal, unlike anything else in town.

**Fulio's Pastaria** — Italian/Tuscan, handmade pasta, downtown. $$. Solid sit-down dinner, full bar.

**Inferno** — Wood-fired pizza and lounge. $$. Good casual dinner-and-drinks option.

**Noodz** — Vietnamese, pho and rice bowls. $–$$. Quick, tasty, good for a light lunch.

**Street 14 Café** — Café + dinner spot at 14th & Commercial; strong coffee and a full menu (see Coffee below).

### Coffee & Bakeries

**Blue Scorcher Bakery & Café** — *worker-owned, the local morning spot*
- **Address:** 1493 Duane St (next to Fort George) · **Price:** $
- **Why:** Organic artisan breads, pastries, breakfast, kids' play corner. Cooperatively owned. The Astoria breakfast move — pair with a Fort George visit right next door.

**Street 14 Café** — 14th & Commercial. Widely called the best espresso on the north coast; roasts in-house. Also does dinner.

**Coffee Girl** — On Pier 39, in the old cannery over the water. Great espresso + a view of the river and sea lions. Casual pastries and paninis.

**Astoria Coffeehouse & Bistro** — 243 11th St. Coffee, cocktails, and a full bistro menu; reliable all-day option downtown.

## Attractions

### In Town

**Astoria Column** ★
- 125-ft tower atop Coxcomb Hill with a spiral **164-step** climb to a 360° view of the river, bridge, ocean, and Coast Range.
- **Tradition:** buy a **balsa-wood glider** ($1–2) in the gift shop and launch it from the top.
- **Hours:** grounds dawn–dusk (park closes 10 PM) · **Parking:** annual pass **~$10/vehicle** (raised from $5 in 2026), good the rest of the calendar year · **Web:** [astoriacolumn.org](https://astoriacolumn.org/)

**Columbia River Maritime Museum** ★ — *one of Oregon's best museums*
- Exhibits on the treacherous Columbia Bar ("Graveyard of the Pacific"), Coast Guard rescues, a lightship you can board, and a 3D theater.
- **Address:** 1792 Marine Dr · **Hours:** daily 9:30 AM–5 PM · **Admission:** ~$18 adult · **Web:** [crmm.org](https://www.crmm.org/)
- Easily 1.5–2 hours; the standout indoor attraction and a perfect rainy-morning stop.

**Flavel House Museum** — ornate 1885 Queen Anne Victorian mansion built by a river-bar pilot, restored to period. Part of the Clatsop County Historical Society.
- **Admission:** $7 adult (or the **Adventure in History Pass, $14 adult**, covering Flavel House + Heritage Museum + Oregon Film Museum) · **Web:** [astoriamuseums.org](https://astoriamuseums.org/)

**Oregon Film Museum** — in the old county jail (which appears in *The Goonies*' opening). Fun, small, celebrates films shot in Oregon. $6 adult or included in the Adventure in History Pass.

**Astoria Riverwalk & Trolley** — a flat **6.4-mile paved trail** along the Columbia. The restored 1913 **"Old 300" trolley** runs the waterfront (seasonal, ~$1–2 per ride) — a charming, cheap way to see the river, canneries, and sea lions.

**Astoria–Megler Bridge** — the **longest continuous truss bridge in North America** (4.1 mi), soaring into Washington. Iconic to photograph from the Riverwalk; you drive it if continuing north.

**Cathedral Tree Trail** — a **0.7-mile** forested hike to a massive old-growth **Sitka spruce**; connects up toward the Astoria Column if you want to earn the view on foot.

**The Goonies House** — the private home from the 1985 film, on Hillcrest/38th St. **Note it's a private residence** — view respectfully from the street, don't trespass. The town leans into the *Goonies* connection (Oregon Film Museum, occasional "Goonies Day" events).

### Nearby

**Fort Stevens State Park & the Peter Iredale Shipwreck** ★ (20–25 min west)
- The rusted skeleton of the **Peter Iredale**, a 1906 shipwreck, sits right on a wild Pacific beach — Astoria's best beach and photo op, especially at **low tide**.
- Also: WWII-era gun batteries and bunkers (Fort Stevens was **the only mainland US military installation fired on during WWII**, by a Japanese submarine in 1942), Coffenbury Lake, miles of trails, and the Clatsop Spit where river meets ocean.
- **Day-use parking permit required** ($5/day at the machine, or Oregon's $30/year pass) · **Web:** [stateparks.oregon.gov — Fort Stevens](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=129)

**Fort Clatsop / Lewis & Clark National Historical Park** (15 min south)
- Replica of the fort where the Lewis & Clark expedition **wintered in 1805–06**. Ranger talks, living-history demos, forest and river trails.
- **Admission:** ~$10/adult (7-day pass) · **Hours:** daily 9 AM–5 PM · **Web:** [nps.gov/lewi](https://www.nps.gov/lewi/)

## Nearby Beaches

Astoria itself is on the **river**, not the ocean — for actual surf you head west or south.

| Beach | Drive | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Fort Stevens / Clatsop Spit** | 20–25 min W | Wild, wide, uncrowded. Peter Iredale wreck, bunkers, dunes. The closest real ocean beach. |
| **Gearhart** | 25 min S | Upscale, quiet residential town. Long calm beach, golf, Pacific Way Bakery. Low-key and lovely. |
| **Seaside** | 18 min S | Classic tourist boardwalk ("the Prom"), arcades, saltwater taffy, promenade turnaround. Busier and more commercial — good for a fun couple of hours, less for peace. |

## Day Trip from Manzanita (~1h30m)

A long but doable day trip — best on Sunday Aug 24. Leave Manzanita by 9 AM.

- **Bowpicker Fish & Chips** — lunch first (cash only, sells out, arrive at open ~11am)
- **Columbia River Maritime Museum** — 1.5–2 hrs, one of Oregon's best ($18 adult)
- **Astoria Riverwalk + trolley** — ride to Pier 39, see sea lions at the East Mooring Basin
- **Astoria Column** — climb 164 steps, buy a balsa glider and launch it from the top
- **Fort George Brewery** — dinner and a pint; no reservations, great atmosphere
- **Astoria Sunday Market** (Sundays 10am–3pm) — only if you arrive early enough
- **Peter Iredale shipwreck at Fort Stevens** (20 min west) — add if you have time; best at low tide

## Practical Notes

- **Weather:** August is Astoria's best month — highs ~68–72°F, driest of the year. Still bring a light layer; river mornings are cool and fog can roll in.
- **Parking:** downtown is walkable and metered/free is easy. Park once and walk. The Column and Fort Stevens need paid day passes.
- **Cash:** carry some — **Bowpicker is cash-only**, and a few small spots prefer it.
- **Pace:** it's a compact city — you can hit the highlights in 2 days without rushing. Rain-day proof thanks to the museums and pubs.
- **Tide chart:** check before the Peter Iredale visit — the wreck and beach are best at low tide.
- **Sea lions:** hundreds haul out on the docks near the East Mooring Basin (near the Riverwalk east end) — loud, smelly, hilarious. Free show.
