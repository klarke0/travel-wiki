# Manzanita

![Manzanita Beach Oregon](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/ad/Manzanita_Beach_-_Oregon.jpg)
*Photo: Doug Kerr / CC BY-SA 2.0*

Manzanita is the anti–Cannon Beach: a quiet, locals-oriented village fronting seven miles of hard-packed, uncrowded sand. Twenty minutes south of the Haystack Rock crowds, it trades galleries and gift shops for a single walkable main street (Laneda Ave), a handful of genuinely good restaurants, and easy reach to Oswald West State Park, Nehalem Bay, and Wheeler. In August, when Cannon Beach is bumper-to-bumper by 10 AM, Manzanita still feels like a secret.

**Distance from Portland:** ~1h45m–2h | **Vibe:** Quiet, locals-oriented | **Beach:** 7 miles, uncrowded

**Trip dates:** August 23–25, 2026 (2 nights) · 2 adults
**Route:** US-26 West over the Coast Range → US-101 South to Manzanita. Add 30–45 min on summer Friday afternoons; leave Portland by 8 AM Saturday.
**Last updated:** 2026-07-14

## At a Glance

| | Manzanita | Cannon Beach |
|---|---|---|
| Crowds | Quiet, locals-oriented | Overrun in summer |
| Parking | Free, easy | Fills by 10 AM, chaotic |
| Rental prices | 20–30% cheaper | Peak pricing all summer |
| Restaurants | Small, intimate, close early | More options, longer waits |
| Beach | 7 miles, usually spacious | 3 miles, busy near Haystack |
| Things to do | Nehalem Bay, Wheeler, Oswald West | Ecola SP, Haystack Rock, galleries |
| Vibe | Low-key, residential, peaceful | Touristy, charming, busy |

**Bottom line:** Cannon Beach has more attractions, but Manzanita delivers a better *experience* in August. Haystack Rock is a 20-minute drive north if you want to see it — do it early, then retreat.

## Restaurants

> Most kitchens close by 8–9 PM and open only 4–5 days/week. Arrive at opening or eat off-peak, and always confirm hours the week of the trip — Manzanita restaurants adjust seasonally on short notice.

**Yolk** ⭐ — *Modern American breakfast · $ · 503-368-9655*
- **Hours:** Fri–Mon 8 AM–2 PM only
- **Must-order:** lemon-ricotta pancakes; lamb burger with feta
- The best breakfast in town (TripAdvisor 4.8/5). Small room, fills fast — go at open. Closed Tue–Thu, so plan Saturday or Monday around it.
- 503 Laneda Ave · [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Yolk+Manzanita+Oregon)

## Attractions & Activities

### Beaches

**Manzanita Beach** — Seven miles of hard-packed crescent sand, rarely crowded. Ideal for walking, kite flying, beachcombing, and sunsets. Tide pooling at low tide twice daily. **Sneaker waves are real — never turn your back on the ocean near rocks.** Free, easy parking at the Laneda Ave beach access.

![Manzanita from Neahkahnie Mountain](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/81/Manzanita_from_Neahkahanie_Mountain_-_panoramio.jpg)
*Photo: zak11527 / CC BY-SA 3.0*

**Short Sand Beach ("Shorty's")** — In Oswald West State Park (~15 min north). A half-mile walk through old-growth Sitka spruce to a sheltered cove; the area's premier surf beach, with a beachside picnic area. See hiking notes below for parking and access.

### Hiking

**Cape Falcon Loop** (Oswald West State Park) — ~5 miles round trip, moderate. Temperate rainforest opening onto an exposed headland with panoramic ocean views. One of the best coastal hikes in Oregon. Muddy and rooty year-round; wear real shoes.

**Neahkahnie Mountain Trail** — 3–5 miles, moderate, 900–1,000 ft gain. Panoramic views over Nehalem Bay and the coastline. Trailhead just south of Oswald West on Hwy 101.

**Oswald West State Park logistics** — Parking is **$10/day** (Oregon State Park day-use, or free with an annual pass). Lots fill fast: **arrive before 9 AM or after 4 PM.**
- ⚠️ **Bridge status (2025–2026):** The **Necarney Creek suspension bridge is out — possibly permanently** — and the Upper Short Sand Creek Trail bridge has failed. This affects the Oregon Coast Trail through the park and some connecting routes. Short Sand Beach and the core Cape Falcon Trail remain accessible, but **verify current conditions before you go**: [Oswald West notices](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=v.notices&parkId=139) · [park profile](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=139) · park info 1-800-551-6949.

### Water Activities

**Nehalem Bay State Park** (~4 mi south) — Kayaking, crabbing, clamming, fishing, and a 1.8-mile forested bike path with bay views. Wildlife: deer, elk, shorebirds. [Park profile](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=142)
- **Horseback riding on the beach** — available Memorial Day–Labor Day. ⚠️ **Closed Sundays in July–August.** Reserve well in advance at **971-237-6653**; confirm weekend availability for Aug 24 (a Sunday — likely no rides).

**Wheeler Marina** (278 Marine Dr, Wheeler) — kayaks, canoes, SUPs, and motorboats for crabbing on the Nehalem River and Bay. [wheelermarina.com](https://wheelermarina.com/)

**Kelly's Brighton Marina** (just north of Manzanita on Nehalem Bay) — salmon fishing and crabbing, all equipment provided.
- *Note:* peak Dungeness crab season is Sept–Feb; August crabs run leaner but it's still a fun outing.

![Nehalem Bay sunset](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/04/Nehalem_bay_sunset.JPG)

### Farmers Market & Shopping

**Manzanita Farmers Market** — every Friday 4–7 PM through mid-September at Underhill Plaza. Local produce, pottery, chocolate, jewelry, live music. ⚠️ **Your trip (Sun–Tue) misses it** — the nearest markets are Fri Aug 21 (day before you arrive) and Fri Aug 28 (after you leave).

**Laneda Ave shopping:**
- **Cloud & Leaf Bookstore** — excellent independent bookshop
- **Manzanita Sweets** — candy and taffy
- **The Winery at Manzanita** — dog-friendly courtyard, open daily 12–7 PM
- **RustyMoose** *(formerly MacGregor's Whiskey Bar)* — whiskey bar, 200+ bottles (387 Laneda Ave)

**Nehalem antiques** (~5 min south): Nehalem Riverside Trading Company, Pete's Antiques, North Coast Antique Mall, Little White Church Antiques.

### Day Trips

- **Cannon Beach** (20 min north) — worth a morning for Haystack Rock; don't linger in August crowds.
- **Tillamook Creamery** (35 min south) — free self-guided tour + legendary ice cream. A genuine Oregon institution.
- **Rockaway Beach** (10 min south) — Old Oregon Smokehouse for clam chowder and crab cakes.

### August Events

- **Manzanita Farmers Market** — Fridays only · ⚠️ Aug 23 is a Sunday — trip misses it. Prior: Fri Aug 21. Next: Fri Aug 28.
- **Oregon Coast Scenic Railroad Moonlight Run** — Aug 22 & 29. Over-21 evening train, Wheeler → Rockaway Beach. [oregoncoastscenic.org](https://oregoncoastscenic.org/train-rides/moonlight-excursion/)
- **Pearl & Oyster Music Festival** — Aug 22–23, Bay City (~25 mi south)

## Practical Notes

- **Cell service is patchy** — download offline maps before leaving Portland; don't rely on data.
- **Book the rental now** — in July for an August trip, hot-tub availability is already slim.
- **7-night minimum hurdle** — call agencies directly; they sometimes fill gap weeks with 2-night bookings.
- **Horseback riding** — reserve at Oregon Beach Rides [(503) 801-7433](tel:+15038017433); closed Sundays in July/Aug. **Aug 24 is a Monday — rides should be available.** Book ahead.
- **The Salmonberry** — walk-in only; arrive at opening (4 PM) on weekends.
- **Oswald West bridges** — Necarney Creek bridge out (possibly permanent) and Upper Short Sand Creek bridge failed as of 2025–2026. Verify trail status before planning the hike.
- **Everything closes early** — most kitchens shut by 8–9 PM and several close Wed–Thu. Build the day around confirmed hours.
