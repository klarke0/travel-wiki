# Cannon Beach

![Haystack Rock at Cannon Beach](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1603232644162-5ac9e5f0f376?fm=jpg&q=80&w=2000&auto=format&fit=crop)

The Oregon coast's headline act: a charming, gallery-lined village fronting three miles of wide sand and the unmistakable 235-foot silhouette of Haystack Rock. It's the most iconic — and in August, the most crowded — town on the north coast, so the trick is booking early and moving early. Base here for tidepools at the rock, the old-growth trails of Ecola and Oswald West, and Arch Cape's quieter beaches five minutes south.

**Distance from Portland:** ~1.5–2h (2.5–3h on summer weekends) via US-26 W → US-101 S
**Vibe:** Iconic, charming, art-forward, genuinely busy in summer
**Beach:** 3 miles of wide, walkable sand
**Trip dates:** August 23–25, 2026 (2 nights, 2 adults) · **Hot tub required**

**Last updated:** 2026-07-14

## At a Glance — Cannon Beach vs. Manzanita

| | Cannon Beach | Manzanita (20 min S) |
|---|---|---|
| Vibe | Iconic, polished, gallery town | Low-key, sleepy, local |
| Summer crowds | Heavy — peak coast destination | Moderate |
| Rental prices | Higher (premium market) | ~20–30% cheaper |
| Dining | Wide range, fine dining present | Small but strong |
| Signature sight | Haystack Rock + tidepools | Neahkahnie Mountain views |
| Parking | Genuinely difficult in August | Easier |

> **Budget move:** Arch Cape (5 mi south of Cannon Beach) and Manzanita both run cheaper than Cannon Beach proper for the same beach quality. Arch Cape sections below.

## Attractions & Activities

### Haystack Rock & Tidal Pools

235-foot basalt sea stack and protected Oregon Marine Garden — one of the largest intertidal structures in the US. Free, open beach.

- **Tidepools:** sea stars, green anemones, crabs, chitons, nudibranchs. **Arrive 1–2 hours before low tide** and target a morning low tide (see Low Tide Timing below). Walk only on bare rock/sand; no climbing above the barnacle line, no collecting.
- **⚠️ Puffins — manage expectations:** Tufted puffins are present April–August and **depart by late August**. An Aug 23–25 trip is at the very tail — puffins may already be gone. Best viewing is **8–11am**, looking toward the grassy patch on the **northeast side** of the rock. Murres, cormorants, and pigeon guillemots stay year-round.
- **Haystack Rock Awareness Program (HRAP):** free interpreters with spotting scopes and microscopes at the base during daytime low tides, roughly Feb–Oct. [haystackrockawareness.com](https://www.haystackrockawareness.com/) · (503) 436-8064

### Ecola State Park

$5 day-use parking (or Oregon State Parks pass). Dawn–dusk. Head north on Ecola Park Road to the fee station. [stateparks.oregon.gov (parkId 136)](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=136)

> **⚠️ Partial closure (summer 2026):** The **west section of the Clatsop Loop Trail is CLOSED** due to an active landslide (reported April 2026) — no reopening date. Ecola Point, Indian Beach, and Crescent Beach remain open; the loop can only be walked one-way inland. ("Cape Falcon" is in Oswald West, not here.)

| Destination | Distance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ecola Point → Indian Beach (OCT) | 1.5 mi one way | Dramatic coastal views |
| Ecola Point → Crescent Beach | 1.25 mi one way | Steep; secluded, low-tide caves |
| Tillamook Head / Hiker's Camp | Varies | Views of "Terrible Tilly" lighthouse |

- **Indian Beach:** beginner-friendly surf, less crowded.
- **Parking:** small lots at Ecola Point and Indian Beach fill fast — **arrive before 10am** on August weekends; narrow winding road, no RVs/trailers.

### Beaches

- **Tolovana Beach State Recreation Site** — South-end access to Haystack Rock; **free parking**, playground, restrooms. Walk north along the beach to the rock. Lot fills early on sunny weekends — good parking fallback if you arrive early. [parkId 141](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=141)
- **Arcadia Beach** — Just south; rock formations, limited parking, arrive early.
- **Chapman Point (north end)** — Viewpoint over Crescent Beach and offshore Bird Rocks (murre colonies). Reached by walking north along the beach around the dune past Ecola Creek — **tide-dependent and only safe near low tide; don't get caught by an incoming tide.** Not a drive-up viewpoint.
- **❌ Hug Point** — **FULLY CLOSED** since Nov 2025 (severe erosion destroyed the stairs and bypass trail). No reopening date; sea caves and stagecoach tracks are not accessible. **Remove from itinerary.**

### Hiking — Oswald West State Park

15–20 min south on US-101. **New parking fee (since Oct 2025): $10/day OR residents, $12 out-of-state** (waived if you walk/bike/bus in). Old-growth Sitka spruce and Douglas fir — a genuine highlight. [parkId 139](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=139)

| Trail | Distance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Short Sand Beach ("Shorty's") | ~0.5 mi from lot | Sheltered surf cove, tidepools, family-friendly |
| Cape Falcon | ~2.5 mi one way | Exposed headland views over Smuggler's Cove; muddy |
| Neahkahnie Mountain | Moderate, ~800 ft gain | Big panoramic summit ocean vistas |

**Parking:** US-101 lots fill very early and roadside parking is enforced — **arrive before 9am or after 4pm.**

### Shopping & Galleries

All-independent downtown along **Hemlock Street** — walkable end-to-end in ~20 min, mostly open 10am–5/6pm.

- **White Bird Gallery** (251 N Hemlock) — contemporary fine art + craft, since 1971.
- **Icefire Glassworks** — live glassblowing demos, marine-life glass.
- **Northwest by Northwest Gallery** — established regional artists.
- **Bruce's Candy Kitchen** (since 1963) — saltwater taffy made in-house; the pink-and-white landmark.
- **Cannon Beach History Center & Museum** — 1387 S Spruce St · **Wed–Sat 11am–4pm** · **by donation** · ~30–45 min · Lewis & Clark and USS *Shark* cannon history. **Note: closed Sun Aug 23 and Mon Aug 24 — visit Saturday if you want it.** [cbhistory.org](https://www.cbhistory.org/)

Park once and walk — downtown parking is scarce midday.

No marquee festival lands on Aug 23–25 (the big Sandcastle Contest is a June event). This weekend's rhythm is driven by **tides, not events** — build mornings around low tide. Recurring summer programming still running:

## Suggested Itinerary

### Sunday, Aug 23 — Arrive & settle
- **Depart Portland by 8am** to beat US-26 weekend traffic.
- Late-morning check the day's low tide; if it's a morning low, head straight to **Haystack Rock tidepools** before parking fills.
- Lunch at **Ecola Seafoods** (fish & chips) or **Mo's**.
- Afternoon: browse **Hemlock St galleries** and Bruce's Candy Kitchen; catch the **History Center** (open Sat) if you want it.
- Dinner: **The Wayfarer** (reserve a window seat) or **Driftwood**.
- Evening soak in the rental hot tub.

### Monday, Aug 24 — Parks day
- **Arrive at Ecola State Park before 10am** (lot fills) — Ecola Point views, walk to Indian Beach.
- Or drive 20 min south to **Oswald West** for **Short Sand Beach** and **Cape Falcon** (arrive before 9am).
- Lunch: **Public Coast** or **Bill's Tavern** back in town.
- Afternoon: beach time; if low tide is afternoon, walk to **Chapman Point** (mind the incoming tide).
- Dinner: **Stephanie Inn Dining Room** (reserve ahead) for a splurge, or **The Irish Table** (arrive early, no reservations).

### Tuesday, Aug 25 — Checkout
- Early **Cannon Beach Bakery** run (Haystack Bread + morning buns before they sell out) or **Sea Level Bakery**.
- One last low-tide beach walk to Haystack Rock.
- Catch the **Tuesday Farmers Market** only if you extend; otherwise head out mid-morning to beat traffic.

## Practical Notes

- **Parking is the real challenge in August.** Weekends are the worst. Arrive before 10am or after 3pm. Best lots: Spruce St, 2nd St near the Information Center, Hemlock & Gower (midtown), Tolovana Wayside (south, free). Meters ~$2/hr; lots ~$5–10/day. A summer shuttle runs from Tolovana into downtown (~$1/ride, [NW Oregon Transit](https://www.nworegontransit.org/)).
- **Park fees stack:** Ecola $5/day; Oswald West $10/day (OR resident). An Oregon State Parks annual pass covers both if you'll visit repeatedly.
- **August weather:** highs ~67–68°F, lows ~52°F, very little rain. Morning marine fog burns off by noon; 10–15°F drop and wind after sunset. Pack a light jacket/hoodie, wind-blocker, and sunscreen (UV stays high through thin cloud).
- **Two closures to plan around:** **Hug Point is fully closed**, and **Ecola's Clatsop Loop west section is closed** — neither is accessible in summer 2026.
- **Low Tide Timing:** critical for tidepools and Chapman Point access. Pull the exact Aug 23–25 chart before you go — [NOAA Tide Predictions for Cannon Beach](https://www.cannonbeach.org/things-to-do/beaches-and-parks/tide-charts/) — and aim mornings at the low tide.
- **Manzanita is 20 min south** if you want a quieter, cheaper comparison base — see [[manzanita]].
