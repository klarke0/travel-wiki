## Attractions & Activities

### Haystack Rock

235-foot basalt sea stack, Oregon Marine Garden (protected tidepool zone). One of the largest intertidal structures in the US.

- **Tidepools**: Sea stars, green anemones, crabs, chitons, limpets, nudibranchs. Arrive 1–2 hours before low tide. Walk only on bare rock or sand.
- **Puffins**: Tufted puffins nest April–July, **depart mid-to-late August** — don't plan around them for Aug 23–25. Other seabirds (murres, cormorants, pigeon guillemots) remain year-round.
- **HRAP interpretive programs**: Free, mid-February through October during low tides. Spotting scopes, microscopes, live specimens. [haystackrockawareness.com](https://www.haystackrockawareness.com/)
- **Rules**: No climbing above barnacle line; no collecting; dogs leashed within 300 yards.

### Ecola State Park

$5 day-use permit required. Head north on Ecola Park Road, 1.5 miles to fee station. **Note:** Clatsop Loop Trail closed by landslide March 2026; day-use areas reopened April 2026 — confirm trail status at [stateparks.oregon.gov](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=136).

| Trail | Distance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ecola Point → Indian Beach (OCT) | 1.5 mi one way | Dramatic coastal views, Oregon Coast Trail |
| Ecola Point → Crescent Beach | 1.25 mi one way | Steep, rugged; secluded beach with low-tide caves and seasonal waterfall |
| Tillamook Head Trail | 6+ mi from Indian Beach | Views of Tillamook Rock Lighthouse ("Terrible Tilly") |

- **Indian Beach**: Beginner-friendly surf spot, less crowded.
- **Crescent Beach**: Rarely visited, surrounded by headlands; low-tide caves and tunnels.
- **Parking tip**: Arrive before 9 AM — lot fills and rangers turn people away by 10–11 AM on summer weekends.

### Oswald West State Park

10–15 min south of Cannon Beach on US-101. $10/day Oregon State Park Pass. Old-growth Sitka spruce and Douglas fir — a genuine highlight.

| Trail/Destination | Distance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Short Sand Beach ("Shorty's") | 0.5 mi from parking | Sheltered cove, Blumenthal Falls at north end, tidepools, popular surf spot |
| Cape Falcon Trail | 4.5 mi RT | Exposed headland views over Smuggler's Cove; muddy — closed-toe shoes |
| Elk Flats Trail | 3 mi | Features suspension bridge |
| Neahkahnie Mountain | Moderate | 800 ft elevation gain, panoramic views |

**Parking tip**: Lot fills fast on summer weekends — arrive before 9 AM or after 4 PM.

### Other Beaches & Natural Areas

- **Arcadia Beach**: Just south of town; interesting rock formations; limited parking — arrive early
- **Hug Point State Recreation Site**: 5 min south; sea caves and historic stagecoach wheel tracks at low tide. *Check access status — severe erosion closure reported March 2026.*
- **Chapman Beach**: North of town, quiet; adjacent to Les Shirley Park nature trails
- **Tolovana Beach State Recreation Site**: South end; kite flying, picnicking, paddleboarding, playground, Haystack Rock views
- **John Yeon State Natural Site**: Secluded, uncrowded beach option

### Wildlife

- **Elk viewing**: 5 designated elk viewing locations in the Cannon Beach area
- **Whale watching**: Not peak season (spring = March–April, winter = December–January) but occasional gray whale sightings year-round
- **Birdwatching**: Chapman Point (north end) has Bird Rocks with murre colonies

## Practical Info

### August Weather

| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| High temp | ~67–68°F |
| Low temp | ~52°F |
| Rain chance | Very low; less than 1 inch for the month (~9% of days) |
| Morning | Marine fog/layer common until 10–11 AM, burns off by noon |
| Afternoon | Usually sunny and pleasant |
| Evening | 10–15°F drop after sunset; wind picks up |

**Pack**: Light jacket/hoodie for morning and evening; wind-blocker for beach; sunscreen (UV stays high on partly cloudy days); layers.

### Parking

Limited; August weekends are the most congested. Arrive before 10 AM or after 3 PM.

- **Best lots**: Spruce St, 2nd St near Information Center, Hemlock & Gower (midtown), Tolovana Wayside (south)
- **Shuttle option**: Park at Tolovana Beach State Park → free/cheap summer shuttle into downtown ($1/ride, NW Oregon Transit: [nworegontransit.org](https://www.nworegontransit.org/))
- **Meters**: ~$2/hr | **Lots**: ~$5–10/day
- **Ecola State Park**: $5 separate day-use permit
- **Oswald West**: $10/day Oregon State Park Pass

### August Events

No special events Aug 23–25 specifically. Major events:
- Sandcastle Contest: June 20, 2026 *(already passed)*
- Cottage Tour: September 11–13, 2026 *(after trip)*
- Stormy Weather Arts Festival: November 6–8, 2026 *(after trip)*

Recurring summer programming is active: Tuesday Farmer's Market, Thursday Buskers Concerts, Movies in the Park.

### Low Tide Timing

Critical for tidepools, Hug Point stagecoach tracks, and Crescent Beach caves. Check [NOAA Tides](https://tidesandcurrents.noaa.gov/) for Aug 23–25 tide chart before the trip.

## Trip Tips for August

1. **Book lodging NOW** — August weekend rentals go 3–6 months out
2. **Reserve restaurants** — Wayfarer, Stephanie Inn, Anna's Table especially
3. **Arrive early both days** — 9 AM for beach and parks before crowds and parking fills
4. **Ecola State Park first thing** — rangers turn people away from the parking lot by 10–11 AM
5. **Oswald West: before 9 AM or after 4 PM** — Short Sand Beach lot fills fast
6. **Park once and walk** — downtown is compact; don't move the car mid-day
7. **Check tide chart** — plan tidepool and cave visits around low (ideally minus) tides
8. **Depart Portland by 8 AM** — beat the US-26 weekend traffic
