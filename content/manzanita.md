# Manzanita

**Summary**: Manzanita is a quiet, locals-oriented beach town 90 miles from Portland — 20 minutes south of Cannon Beach with a completely different vibe. Seven miles of uncrowded beach, great restaurants on Laneda Ave, and easy access to Nehalem Bay, Oswald West State Park, and Wheeler. The anti-Cannon Beach.

**Trip dates**: August 23–25, 2026 (2 nights) | 2 adults
**Drive from Portland**: ~1h45m–2h (US-26 West → US-101 South). Add 30–45 min on summer Friday afternoons. Leave by 8 AM Saturday.
**Route**: US-26 West over Coast Range → US-101 South to Manzanita

**Last updated**: 2026-07-14

## Manzanita vs. Cannon Beach

| | Manzanita | Cannon Beach |
|---|---|---|
| Crowds | Quiet, locals-oriented | Overrun in summer |
| Parking | Free, easy | Fills by 10 AM, chaotic |
| Rental prices | 20–30% cheaper | Peak pricing all summer |
| Restaurants | Small, intimate, closes early | More options, longer waits |
| Beach | 7 miles, usually spacious | 3 miles, busy near Haystack |
| Things to do | Nehalem Bay, Wheeler, Oswald West | Ecola SP, Haystack Rock, galleries |
| Vibe | Low-key, residential, peaceful | Touristy, charming, busy |

**Bottom line:** Cannon Beach has more attractions but Manzanita has a better *experience* in August. Haystack Rock is 20 min north if you want to see it.

## Attractions & Activities

### Manzanita Beach
Seven miles of hard-packed crescent beach — rarely crowded. Great for walking, kite flying, beachcombing, watching sunsets. Tide pooling at low tide twice daily. **Sneaker waves are real — never turn your back on the ocean near rocks.**

### Oswald West State Park (~15 min north on Hwy 101)
- **Short Sand Beach ("Shorty's")**: Half-mile walk through old-growth Sitka spruce to a sheltered cove — the premiere surf beach in the area. Picnic area on the beach.
- **Cape Falcon Loop**: ~5 miles round trip, moderate. Temperate rainforest → exposed headland with panoramic ocean views. One of the best coastal hikes in Oregon.
- **Parking**: $10/day Oregon State Park Pass. Fills fast — arrive before 9 AM or after 4 PM.
- ⚠️ **Bridge closures (2025–2026)**: Upper Short Sand Creek Trail bridge failed, Necarney Creek suspension bridge removed. Verify current status at [stateparks.oregon.gov](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=139) before going.

### Nehalem Bay State Park (~4 mi south)
- **Horseback riding on the beach**: Available Memorial Day–Labor Day. ⚠️ Closed Sundays in July–August — confirm weekend availability. **Reserve at 971-237-6653** well in advance.
- Kayaking, crabbing, clamming, fishing
- 1.8-mile forested bike path with bay views
- Wildlife: deer, elk, shorebirds
- [stateparks.oregon.gov](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&parkId=142)

### Neahkahnie Mountain Trail
3–5 miles, moderate, 900–1,000 ft gain. Panoramic views of Nehalem Bay and coast. Trailhead just south of Oswald West on Hwy 101.

### Wheeler — Kayaking & Crabbing (~8 mi south, 12 min)
- **Wheeler Marina** (278 Marine Dr): Kayaks, canoes, SUPs, motorboats for crabbing on Nehalem River and Bay. [wheelermarina.com](https://wheelermarina.com/)
- **Kelly's Brighton Marina** (just north of Manzanita on Nehalem Bay): Salmon fishing and crabbing, all equipment provided.
- Note: Peak Dungeness crab season is September–February; August crabs are leaner.

### Manzanita Farmers Market
Every Friday 4–7 PM through mid-September at Underhill Plaza. Local produce, pottery, chocolate, jewelry, live music. **Aug 23 is a Friday — perfect timing.**

### Shopping on Laneda Ave
- **Cloud & Leaf Bookstore** — excellent independent
- **Manzanita Sweets** — candy and taffy
- **The Winery at Manzanita** — dog-friendly outdoor courtyard, barrel fire pits, locally-made wine, open daily 12–7pm
- **MacGregor's Whiskey Bar** — 200+ bottles, charcuterie boards (387 Laneda Ave)

### Nehalem Antiques (~5 min south)
- Nehalem Riverside Trading Company, Pete's Antiques, North Coast Antique Mall, Little White Church Antiques

### Nearby Day Trips
- **Cannon Beach** (20 min north) — worth a morning for Haystack Rock; don't linger in August crowds
- **Tillamook Creamery** (35 min south) — free self-guided tour + legendary ice cream; genuine Oregon institution
- **Rockaway Beach** (10 min south) — Old Oregon Smokehouse for clam chowder and crab cakes

### August Events
- **Manzanita Farmers Market** — Aug 23 (Friday) 4–7 PM
- **Oregon Coast Scenic Railroad Moonlight Run** — Aug 22 & 29. Over-21 evening train, Wheeler to Rockaway Beach. [oregoncoastscenic.org](https://oregoncoastscenic.org/train-rides/moonlight-excursion/)
- **Pearl & Oyster Music Festival** — Aug 22–23, Bay City (~25 mi south)

## Suggested 2-Night Itinerary

### Saturday Aug 23
- 7:30 AM — Leave Portland on US-26
- 9:30 AM — Arrive, drop bags, walk the beach
- 10 AM — **Yolk** for breakfast (open Fri–Mon, don't miss it)
- Afternoon — **Oswald West State Park**: Short Sand Beach + Cape Falcon hike (arrive early, parking fills fast)
- 4–7 PM — **Manzanita Farmers Market** on Laneda Ave
- Evening — **Big Wave Cafe** or **Offshore Grill** for dinner
- Night — Hot tub at rental

### Sunday Aug 24
- Morning — **Wanda's Cafe** in Nehalem for breakfast
- Mid-morning — **Horseback riding at Nehalem Bay State Park** (reserve at 971-237-6633; confirm Sunday availability)
- Afternoon — **Wheeler Marina**: kayak Nehalem Bay or try crabbing
- Nehalem antiques shops on the way back
- Evening — **The Salmonberry** in Wheeler (walk-in only, arrive at 4 PM when they open)
- Night — Hot tub + MacGregor's Whiskey Bar nightcap

### Monday Aug 25 (checkout)
- Sunrise beach walk (almost no one out)
- Coffee at Manzanita News & Espresso
- Optional: Tillamook Creamery on the way home (take US-101 south → US-6 east to Portland)

## Practical Notes

- **Cell service is patchy** — download offline maps before leaving Portland. Don't rely on data.
- **Book rentals now** — if it's July 2026 and this trip is in August, hot tub availability is slim.
- **7-night minimum hurdle**: Call agencies directly — they sometimes fill gap weeks with 2-night bookings.
- **Horseback riding**: Reserve at 971-237-6653. Closed Sundays in July/August — confirm for Aug 24.
- **The Salmonberry**: Walk-in only, arrive at opening (4 PM) on weekends.
- **Oswald West bridges**: Both main suspension bridges closed/removed as of 2025–2026. Verify trail status before planning that hike.
